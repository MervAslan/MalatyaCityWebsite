Top 5 Places to Visit in Malatya

Aslantepe Mound

  • Located in Malatya, Turkey, Aslantepe Mound is one of the oldest and most important archaeological sites in the world. This UNESCO World Heritage Site dates back over 6,000 years and was once home to one of the earliest known palaces and bureaucratic systems in history.

  • Visitors to Aslantepe can explore ancient mudbrick structures, admire beautifully preserved wall paintings, and witness the roots of early state formation. The site offers a rare glimpse into the birth of organized government, writing systems, and advanced architecture.

  • Whether you're a history enthusiast or simply looking for a unique cultural experience, Aslantepe invites you to walk through the footsteps of ancient rulers and uncover the secrets of a once-great civilization.

Yeni Mosque

  • Yeni Mosque, also known as the New Mosque, is a stunning example of Ottoman architecture located in the heart of Malatya. Built in the 19th century, this mosque features intricate tile work, elegant arches, and a grand dome that captures the essence of Islamic design.

  • Visitors can admire the mosque's beautiful courtyard, adorned with lush gardens and fountains. The interior is equally impressive, with ornate chandeliers and calligraphic inscriptions that reflect the rich cultural heritage of the region.

  • Whether you're seeking spiritual solace or simply want to appreciate the artistry of Ottoman architecture, Yeni Mosque is a must-visit destination in Malatya.

Levent Valley

  • Levent Valley is a breathtaking natural wonder located in the Malatya region. Known for its stunning landscapes, this valley offers visitors a chance to immerse themselves in nature's beauty.

  • One of the highlights of Levent Valley is its famous glass terrace , which provides panoramic views of the surrounding mountains and valleys. Visitors can walk on the glass floor and experience the thrill of being suspended above the breathtaking scenery.

  • Whether you're an adventure seeker or simply looking to relax in a serene environment, Levent Valley is a must-visit destination that showcases the natural beauty of Malatya.

Somuncu Baba Complex

  • The Somuncu Baba Complex is a significant religious and cultural site located in Darende, Malatya. This complex is dedicated to the revered figure of Somuncu Baba, a 13th-century Sufi saint known for his wisdom and teachings.

  • Visitors can explore the beautifully designed mosque, tomb, and educational center within the complex. The serene atmosphere and stunning architecture make it a peaceful place for reflection and spiritual connection.

  • Whether you're interested in Sufism or simply want to experience the tranquility of this sacred site, the Somuncu Baba Complex is a must-visit destination in Malatya.

Malatya Camera Museum

  • The Malatya Camera Museum is a unique and fascinating destination for photography enthusiasts and history buffs alike. This museum is home to Turkey's largest collection of cameras , showcasing the evolution of photography over the years.

  • Visitors can explore a wide range of cameras, from vintage models to modern digital devices, and learn about the history of photography in Turkey and around the world. The museum also features exhibitions that highlight the art of photography and its impact on culture.

  • Whether you're a professional photographer or simply curious about the world of cameras, the Malatya Camera Museum offers an engaging experience that celebrates the art of capturing moments.
